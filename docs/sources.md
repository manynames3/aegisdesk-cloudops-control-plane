# Sources and Market Signal

This project is designed around current demand for AI governance, cloud-native infrastructure, platform engineering, and FinOps.

## Cloud-Native and AI Infrastructure

CNCF's 2026 cloud native survey reports that Kubernetes has become a foundation for production AI workloads:

- 82% of container users run Kubernetes in production.
- 66% of organizations hosting generative AI models use Kubernetes for some or all inference workloads.
- The report highlights platform engineering, security, and observability as important next steps as AI and cloud-native infrastructure converge.

Source:

- https://www.cncf.io/announcements/2026/01/20/kubernetes-established-as-the-de-facto-operating-system-for-ai-as-production-use-hits-82-in-2025-cncf-annual-cloud-native-survey/

## FinOps and AI Cost Management

The FinOps Foundation's 2026 State of FinOps report identifies AI cost management as a top forward-looking skill:

- AI cost management is the number one skillset teams need to develop.
- 98% of respondents now manage AI spend.
- FinOps is expanding from cloud cost management into broader technology value management across AI, SaaS, licensing, private cloud, and data center.

Source:

- https://data.finops.org/

## How These Sources Shape AegisDesk

AegisDesk focuses on:

- AI cost visibility
- Model routing
- Governance and policy enforcement
- Observability
- Cloud-native deployment path
- Platform-style reusable controls

This is why the project is framed as a CloudOps AI control plane instead of a generic AI assistant.
