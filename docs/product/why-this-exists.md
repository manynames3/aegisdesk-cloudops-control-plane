# Why This Exists

Companies want employees to use AI, but unmanaged AI creates enterprise risk.

Public chat tools are useful for general reasoning, but they do not provide enough control for internal cloud operations workflows where users may expose:

- Production logs
- API keys and secrets
- Customer data
- Access requests
- Incident context
- Architecture details
- Vendor and cost information

AegisDesk exists to answer one practical question:

> How can an enterprise let employees use AI while still controlling privacy, cost, access, tooling, and auditability?

## Problem

Most AI demos stop at the answer. Real enterprises need the control plane around the answer.

For CloudOps and platform teams, the risk is not only inaccurate output. The bigger risks are:

- Users paste secrets into a model.
- AI calls a tool the user is not allowed to use.
- Sensitive requests are sent to the wrong model provider.
- A team cannot explain why an AI action was approved or denied.
- AI spend grows without allocation, budgets, or route visibility.
- Compliance teams cannot prove what happened after the fact.

## Thesis

The next valuable cloud skill is not just "can build with AI." It is:

> Can build AI systems that are secure, observable, cost-aware, policy-driven, and deployable.

AegisDesk is built to demonstrate that skill set.
